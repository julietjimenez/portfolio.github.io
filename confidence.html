Building Confidence in Speaking a Foreign Language

Overview

For this class project we were given an open ended prompt - create an idea’s proof of concept for the emerging technology space. In our case, we decided to explore how virual reality could help those learning a foreign language build confidence in their abilities. To show our proof of concept, we created an experience prototype along with storyboard video.

Project Type

Class Project

Final Deliverables

Experience prototype and storyboard video

Length

1 Month (March 2019 - April 2019

Team

Chris Watkins, Wave Upatising, Sarah Field, and Juliet Jimenez

Role

User research, experience prototyping

Final design concept
Problem

The foreign language learning experience lacks a support system to properly build confidence in students. Our goal was to design an experience that takes language learning to the next level by building an individual's confidence to practice and improve their language skills via virtual reality.

Interviews

Our team interviewed 2 students about their experience going abroad and their journey to adjust to a new culture with a new language. Why interviews? None of us had personal experience speaking a foreign language abroad where confidence in your abilities are key. Additionally, our team was looking to better understand the strategies foreign language learners use to improve their confidence.

Takeaways

  • Ability to speak a language does not directly correlate to improvement in confidence
  • Interactions with locals improves confidence and language skills
  • Predictability in conversation affects confidence

Secondary Research

After the interviews we left a narrowed scope but had two important questions:

  • What would building language speaking confidence in a VR environment look like?
  • How can we connect native speakers and learners?
We conducted one round of comparative analysis and later analyzed the results of a research project being conducted by one of our classmates - YuQing Wu.

Comparative Analysis

We looked at five different platforms designed either for language learning or for community building. From left to right, these are: MondlyAR, ImmerseMe, Crystallize (does not exist anymore), Avakin, and Full Dive VR.

Takeaways

  • Platforms currently tackling this space have either gamified it or built a learning community
  • A study conducted by Culbertson et al. (2016) found that Crystallize helped accelerate the language learning process through contextualization and collaboration

YuQing's Research

Her research was titled A Pilot Study of the Communication Barriers of Mandarin-Speaking International Students in American Colleges. We decided to work with YuQing due to the small number of interview participants we had and the short amount of time for this project. YuQing, however, had interviewed 8 Chinese students about their experience learning English and speaking it as a foreign student in an entirely new culture.

Takeaways

  • Students are more willing to speak English if it is to help others
  • Confirmed our previous research that confidence is necessary to empower learners to speak the foreign language

Experience Prototype

From our research we decided to conduct a roleplaying activity to serve as our experience prototype. Based off our research, we knew that the activity should have 3 components:

  • Encourage collaboration through mentorship
  • Gamify the experience
  • Contextualization through everyday words and scenarios
The activity we conducted was used to emulate what the VR experience would be like. We recruited 6 participants - 3 native speakers of Spanish and 3 people who were learning Spanish. The activity was structured around mentorship and phrased that way from the start to encourage collaboration. The students worked in teams of three to solve clues, in Spanish, together. This is where the gamified experience came in - there were points involved and whoever could solve the clue quickest would win.

Reflection

Although this project was not tool heavy, it was my first time working on a project where the sole focus was user research. I wasn’t able to touch on everything we had done like journey mapping, experience mapping, or conducting a roleplay workshop. However, I played the largest role in conducting secondary and primary research. I learned a lot about designing interview protocols, analyzing research for patterns, and creating actionable design insights.
