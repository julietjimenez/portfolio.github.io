Star Behavioral Health Providers (SBHP)

Overview

Star Behavioral Health Providers (SBHP) is a program under the Military Family Research Institute (MFRI) at Purdue. They offer civilian mental healthcare providers military-based trainings and, after completion, providers are listed on SBHP’s searchable website. During the fall of 2018, MFRI sponsored a class project where myself and 5 other UX students redesigned the services side of their site.

Problem

The current SBHP website is outdated and will not scale well when the program expands outside of Indiana. SBHP came to us in need of a website redesign that will attract new users, keep current users on site longer, and look more visually appealing.

Usability Analysis

Our first step was to identify painpoints of the existing website via heuristic and content analysis. By doing so, we were able to identify what parts of the website we should focus our efforts on to solve the aforementioned issues. We discovered 3 main issues:

  • there were seven pages that could be eliminated by incoorporating content elsewhere
  • all pages appeared text-heavy and could benefit from the incoorporation of visuals
  • the search process was tedious due to limited context and an outdated design

Comparative Analysis

The usability analysis prompted us to complete a comparative analysis. Why comparative instead of competitive? We wanted to see how companies working on similar searchable database models were operating because the search process is the main feature of the SBHP site. So, instead of just looking into competitors, we also looked into platforms like airbnb. Our main takeaways:

  • a search flow that is broken into sections makes the process more intuitive
  • beginning the search process on the home page helps the user find what they want faster
  • location is an important factor to the search process and it should be easily visable in results

We tested our sketches and learned the search process should resemble more of a form than optional features. Users often didn’t select filters that were important to searching for a mental healthcare provider. The reasoning? The filtering system we were using is often found on ecommerce websites implying that they are optional. To solve the issue, we broke the search process into multiple steps of a whole.

Reflection

I completed this project during my first year of UX studies (second year of college). I learned a lot but primarily…

  • Community oriented design is my thing; knowing that my work impacts something larger than profits makes me feel fulfilled and more inspired
  • How to communicate my ideas within and outside my team; agile methods help keep the team on track and storytelling is a vital presentation tool
I enjoyed this project so much that I later applied to MFRI and now work part time during the semester as a Student Researcher!
