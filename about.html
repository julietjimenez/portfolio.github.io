The Backstory

Where I've been

Hey, I'm Juliet. Born in California, spent my childhood in small-town Indiana, and moved to the Chicago 'burbs for my teens (also, briefly lived downtown). I then returned to Indiana for an Undergraduate in User Experience Design at Purdue. So, maybe not Midwest born, but definitely Midwest raised. Part of my design inspiration comes from living in a variety of communities and being exposed to many different lifestyles.

In my teens, I was the political friend. At 16, I interned for 10th Congressional Democrats and my dream was to become a U.S. Senator. I get asked a lot why I never pursued politics, and, if you're wondering, I'd be happy to have a conversation about it. But that's a different story for another time. Ultimately, I found UX and fell in love - partially because design is always political. Also, I didn't have time for a minor in MGMT (more on that in a second) but I've taken almost enough classes to have a minor. What can I say, law just gets me goin'.

Where I'm headed

I'll be graduating a semester ahead of schedule in December 2020 with a degree in UX and minors in Communication and Psychology. My work in class, at my jobs, during sponsored projects, and in internships has made me well versed in user experience, user research, and user interface methodologies. However, I've found user research (or product research) to be the area I'm most interested in. Particulary, conducting work that has a broader purpose than the product itself. This is where my interest in community-oriented design comes into play and you can see this reflected throughout my resume and portfolio. I'm currently on the hunt for an amazing internship for the Summer of 2020. Want to get in touch? Check out my resume for contact info.
